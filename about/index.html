About PrizeWire

The definitive database for sports prize money, player salaries, and tournament payout data. Independent. Verified. Zero bias.

Why We Built This

Sports prize money is one of the most searched and least well-covered topics in sports media. When a major tournament ends, fans want to know: how much did the winner actually get? Most sports sites bury that number, get it wrong, or never update it. PrizeWire was built to fix that — one comprehensive, verified, up-to-date database for every dollar in professional sports.

Our Mission

To be the first place anyone goes when they want to know what an athlete earned, what a tournament paid out, or how prize money is structured in any major sport. We cover NFL, NBA, PGA Tour, Formula 1, Tennis, UFC, Boxing, Football (soccer), and more — and we add new sports as demand grows.

How We Work

Every figure on PrizeWire is sourced from official announcements: governing body press releases, state athletic commission disclosures, league official communications, and verified news reports from primary sources. We do not publish estimates without labeling them clearly as estimates. We do not speculate. When we're unsure, we say so.

When a tournament ends, we publish the payout breakdown as quickly as possible — typically within hours of official confirmation. Our goal is to be faster than any traditional media outlet while being more thorough in the data we present.

Editorial Independence

PrizeWire has no financial relationship with any sports league, team, athlete, governing body, or betting company that influences the data we publish. Advertisers do not control, influence, or preview our content. Our corrections policy is simple: if we get something wrong, we correct it publicly and note the correction in the article.
