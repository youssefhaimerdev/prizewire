Methodology

How we source, verify, and publish every number on PrizeWire.

Primary Sources

All prize money figures are sourced from official primary sources before publication. We do not publish from secondary sources alone. Primary sources include:

  • Official governing body press releases (PGA Tour, NFL, NBA, FIFA, FOM, R&A, USTA, ATP, WTA)
  • State athletic commission disclosures (for UFC, boxing)
  • Official league and tournament websites
  • Player association official salary guides (MLSPA, NFLPA, NBPA)
  • Court filings and official legal documents for disputed contracts

Estimates and Approximations

Some figures — particularly driver salaries in F1, UFC undisclosed bonuses, and certain football contracts — are not publicly confirmed. Where we publish estimates, we label them explicitly with "est.", "approx.", or "~" and cite the sources our estimates are based on. We never present estimates as confirmed figures.

Update Policy

Prize money data is updated as quickly as possible after official announcement — typically within hours. Articles are marked with a "Last updated" date in the source box at the bottom of every article. Significant updates or corrections are noted explicitly within the article text.

Corrections Policy

When we publish an error, we correct it as soon as it is identified. Corrections are: (1) made in the article text, (2) noted with a correction statement at the bottom of the article, and (3) dated. We do not silently edit articles. If you spot an error, please email corrections@prizewire.com — we will respond within 24 hours.

Currency and Exchange Rates

Prize money is reported in the currency it was officially announced in. When we convert currencies for comparison purposes, we note the approximate exchange rate and date used. Currency conversions in comparison tables are approximate and may differ from the exact rate at time of payment.
