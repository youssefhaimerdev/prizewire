MLB's Biggest Contracts in History: Soto $765M, Ohtani $700M — The Record Deals

The 2024-25 off-season produced the two largest contracts in professional sports history within months of each other. Juan Soto's 15-year $765M Mets deal and Shohei Ohtani's 10-year $700M Dodgers deal changed the financial landscape of baseball forever.

Two Historic Contracts in Eight Months

In the span of eight months — from December 2023 to December 2024 — baseball produced the two largest contracts in the history of professional sports. First, Shohei Ohtani signed with the Los Angeles Dodgers for 10 years and $700 million in December 2023, shattering every previous record by more than $270 million. Then, in December 2024, Juan Soto signed with the New York Mets for 15 years and $765 million — surpassing Ohtani's deal to become the largest contract in sports history.

Why Soto's Deal Is Actually Worth More

Though Ohtani's contract is larger in nominal value ($700M vs $765M is close to equal when AAV is considered), Soto's deal is arguably worth more in present value. Ohtani is deferring $680 million to 2034-2043. A dollar paid in 2034 is worth significantly less than a dollar paid today due to inflation and investment returns. The MLB Labor Relations Department calculates Ohtani's contract at approximately $457 million in present-day value — meaning the Dodgers got a significant discount. Soto's deal has zero deferred money — every dollar is paid on schedule, maximizing present value.
