NBA Playoff Prize Money Structure

The NBA distributes playoff prize money from a central pool to all participating teams, scaled by how far each team advances. Unlike the NFL — where bonuses are equal across the whole roster — the NBA system allocates a set per-player share based on the team's playoff round of elimination. All players on a qualifying roster split the pool equally.

For the 2025-26 season, the total NBA playoff prize pool is $24.6 million. The champion's roster earns the largest individual share at $547,000 per player. This is separate from — and in addition to — each player's regular season salary, which continues to be paid during the playoffs.

These Numbers Don't Include Salaries

Context matters: Stephen Curry earns $55.76 million in base salary for 2025-26. His potential $547,000 championship share represents about 1% of his annual contract. For a minimum-salary player earning $1.1 million, however, a championship run adds 50% to their season income. The playoff bonus system disproportionately benefits lower-paid players in percentage terms — but all receive identical dollar amounts.

How NBA Compares to NFL Playoff Bonuses

The NBA champion share ($547,000) is 3.5x the NFL Super Bowl winner's share ($157,000). This reflects both different roster sizes (NBA rosters are 15 players vs NFL's 53+) and different total pool sizes. The NBA also requires fewer playoff games to win a title (16 wins across 4 rounds vs NFL's 4 wins), but the stakes — and the paychecks — are significantly higher per player in basketball.
