Augusta's Record Purse

The 2026 Masters Tournament at Augusta National raised its total prize pool to a record $22.5 million — surpassing the previous record of $21 million set in 2025. For the first time in Masters history, the winner's share exceeded $4.5 million, reflecting Augusta's continued commitment to staying competitive with the PGA TOUR's signature events. The winner's share represents exactly 20% of the total purse, a slightly higher percentage than the TOUR's standard 18%.

Masters vs Other 2026 Majors

At $22.5 million, the 2026 Masters surpasses both The Players Championship ($25M, not a major) and the PGA Championship ($20.5M) in terms of total purse, though The Players remains the single largest event on the PGA TOUR. Augusta National operates independently and sets its own prize money, historically being more conservative than the TOUR — but recent years have seen rapid catch-up growth.

The Masters' Unique Financial Structure

Augusta National Golf Club is one of the most financially secretive institutions in sports. It does not release membership figures, revenue, or profit data. Prize money is one of the few financial figures it publicly discloses. Unlike TOUR events, the Masters is not subject to PGA TOUR commercial regulations — Augusta controls every aspect of the tournament's economics, from parking to broadcasting, and reportedly generates over $100 million in revenue annually from the event itself.
