The Story of the Tournament So Far

Norway Chess 2026 began with the biggest upset of the year: Alireza Firouzja defeated Magnus Carlsen in classical chess for the first time in his career — and did it on Day 1, on Carlsen's home turf in Oslo, playing on an injured ankle. Firouzja had traveled directly from Romania, where he'd withdrawn from the Superbet Classic mid-tournament due to a severe ankle injury, to Oslo to honor his commitment. He played Round 1 with his foot elevated on a second chair.

The win sent shockwaves through the chess world. Carlsen called his own play "just an udder embarrassment" in his post-game confessional. In Round 2, Firouzja beat Praggnanandhaa Rameshbabu in another classical game to move to a perfect 6/6 score — a 3.5-point lead over Gukesh Dommaraju and Wesley So, who sit joint second on 2.5.

Today's Round 3 is the tournament's most anticipated game so far: Firouzja with Black vs Gukesh. The World Champion needs a classical win to close the gap. Firouzja needs a draw to maintain his historic lead.

Who Is Alireza Firouzja?

Born in Iran on June 18, 2003, Alireza Firouzja is a 22-year-old French grandmaster widely regarded as the most exciting player in chess since Magnus Carlsen himself. He became the youngest player ever to reach a 2800 FIDE rating and is the only player since Carlsen to challenge the Norwegian's dominance at the super-elite level. Firouzja qualified for the FIDE Candidates Tournament twice — first by winning the FIDE Grand Swiss in 2021 — and has been a fixture in the world's top 10 players for several years.

His performance at Norway Chess 2026 — two classical wins in the first two rounds, including Carlsen — is the best start any player has made to this elite tournament. If he maintains his form through June 5, Firouzja will win Norway Chess for the first time and establish himself as the undeniable challenger to Carlsen's crown.

Magnus Carlsen's Defending Title

Magnus Carlsen has won Norway Chess an extraordinary seven times (2016, 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2024, 2025) and enters as defending champion. The loss to Firouzja in Round 1 — which he described as "an udder embarrassment" — was only his second classical loss to any player in Norway Chess history. Despite his current 5th-place position, ten rounds remain. Carlsen has come back from poor starts before. But the gap to Firouzja (4.5 points) is significant even early in the double round-robin format.
