PGA Championship 2026: Full Payout — Aaron Rai Claims Record $3,690,000

Aaron Rai became the first Englishman to win the PGA Championship in the stroke play era, shooting a final-round 65 at Aronimink Golf Club to claim a three-shot victory and a record $3,690,000 payday from the tournament's largest-ever purse of $20.5 million.

Full Prize Money Breakdown

The 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club marked the third consecutive year of purse growth, hitting a record $20.5 million — up $1.5 million from Scottie Scheffler's win at Quail Hollow in 2025. Aaron Rai entered the final round tied for second at 4-under before an eagle on the par-5 9th turned the tournament. He birdied 11 to take the lead outright and never looked back, posting a 5-under 65 to finish at 9-under 271, three shots clear of Jon Rahm and 54-hole co-leader Alex Smalley.

With Rahm and Smalley tied for second, their payout was calculated as the average of 2nd and 3rd place money: ($2,214,000 + $1,394,000) ÷ 2 = $1,804,000 each. A total of 82 professionals made the cut; players who missed the cut received $4,300.

How the Purse Compares to Previous Years

The 2026 PGA Championship purse of $20.5 million represents a $3.5 million increase over three years — from $17 million in 2023, to $19 million in 2025 (Scheffler's win), to this record. The winner's 18% share structure has remained consistent, meaning as the purse grows, the winner's check scales proportionally. For comparison, Rory McIlroy won $4.5 million at the Masters 2026 from a $22.5 million purse, making Augusta still the richest Major payout for the champion.

Aaron Rai: Who He Is and What This Win Means

Rai, 31, had previously won three times on the DP World Tour and once on the PGA TOUR, but the PGA Championship represents a landmark payday. Known for wearing two gloves and his ball-striking precision, Rai's 68-foot putt on the 17th green in the final round is already being called one of the most iconic putts in major history. Beyond the $3.69 million check, the win earns him lifetime PGA Championship exemption, five-year major exemptions, and a 750-point FedExCup jump — moving him to 24th on the overall standings.
