Lionel Messi Salary at Inter Miami: How Much Does the MLS Champion Really Earn?

Inter Miami co-owner Jorge Mas confirmed in March 2026 that Lionel Messi earns between $70 million and $80 million per year across all streams — including his MLS contract, equity stake in the club, Apple TV deal, Adidas partnership, and other commercial arrangements. Here's the complete breakdown.

The Complete Inter Miami Deal

Lionel Messi extended his contract with Inter Miami in October 2025, signing a new three-year deal through the end of the 2028 MLS season. His MLS guaranteed compensation, as reported by the MLS Players Association, is $20,450,000 per year — with a base salary of $12 million. This figure is what clubs officially pay into the league salary structure and represents the floor of his Inter Miami earnings.

However, the real number is significantly higher. Inter Miami co-owner Jorge Mas told Bloomberg in March 2026: "I pay Messi — worth every penny — but it's $70 million to $80 million a year. Across everything." That total includes Messi's equity ownership stake in the club, revenue sharing from the Apple TV MLS Season Pass deal, his Adidas partnership, and dozens of other sponsorship arrangements.

The 2025 MLS Cup — A Championship Bonus

Adding to his 2025 earnings, Messi led Inter Miami to the 2025 MLS Cup, defeating the Vancouver Whitecaps 3-1 in the final. The victory was celebrated at the White House in March 2026, where President Trump hosted the Inter Miami squad. MLS Cup champions receive performance bonuses on top of their base contract, though exact figures are not publicly disclosed for MLS. The win made Messi — already an eight-time Ballon d'Or winner and World Cup champion with Argentina — an MLS champion as well.

Messi vs. Ronaldo: The Salary Comparison

The eternal debate has a financial dimension. Ronaldo earns approximately €200 million/year ($208M) in base salary at Al-Nassr — roughly 10x Messi's MLS base salary and 3x his total Inter Miami package. However, the comparison is complex: Messi plays in MLS (a capped league with salary rules), while Ronaldo plays in the Saudi Pro League, which has no meaningful salary cap. Both athletes earn significantly more from commercial deals off the pitch. Messi's Adidas lifetime deal, in particular, is believed to be worth more in long-term value than Ronaldo's Nike partnership.
