Cristiano Ronaldo signed a new two-year contract extension with Al-Nassr in June 2025, securing approximately €200 million per year in base salary — making him the highest-paid athlete in the world. When bonuses, equity stakes, and commercial deals are included, his total annual package approaches €240 million.

The New Al-Nassr Contract (June 2025)

On June 26, 2025, Cristiano Ronaldo signed a new two-year contract extension with Al-Nassr, keeping him in Saudi Arabia until June 2027 — beyond his 42nd birthday. His base salary is approximately €200 million per year (£178 million, ~$208 million), making it the highest annual salary ever paid to an athlete in any sport, surpassing his own previous Al-Nassr deal from 2022.

The contract includes a signing bonus of £24.5 million for the first year, rising to £38 million if the second-year option is triggered. Ronaldo also received a 15% equity stake in Al-Nassr, valued at approximately £33 million — meaning he is now a part-owner of the club he plays for.

How Ronaldo's Salary Compares

To put Ronaldo's earnings in context: the entire PGA Championship purse is $20.5 million — Ronaldo earns that in approximately 37 days on his base salary alone. His daily earnings exceed $570,000. His contract dwarfs the highest NFL and NBA salaries: Patrick Mahomes earns $51M/year; Stephen Curry earns $55.7M/year. Ronaldo earns more in a single week (~€3.9M) than most top European footballers earn in an entire season.
