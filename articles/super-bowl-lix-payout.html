The Philadelphia Eagles defeated the Kansas City Chiefs 40-22 in Super Bowl LIX in New Orleans, with each Eagles player earning $157,000 in playoff bonus money. Here's the complete NFL playoff payout structure and what it means for players at every level.

How NFL Playoff Pay Works

Unlike golf or tennis where prize money goes directly to the individual, NFL playoff bonuses are distributed to all players on the active/practice squad roster of each team — equally, regardless of whether they played a single snap. The NFL and NFLPA negotiate this pool figure in the CBA, and the amounts are fixed per round before the season begins. A team's coaching staff, front office, and management do not receive a share of this specific pool.

For Super Bowl LIX (February 9, 2025 in New Orleans), the Philadelphia Eagles demolished the Kansas City Chiefs 40-22, ending Kansas City's bid for a third consecutive Super Bowl. Every player on the Eagles' winning roster received $157,000 — but that's only the Super Bowl round bonus. Players who ran the full playoff gauntlet earned significantly more by accumulating bonuses at each stage.

What a Full Playoff Run Is Worth

An Eagles player who started from the Wild Card round and went all the way to the championship collected: $41K (Wild Card win) + $54K (Divisional win) + $67K (Conference Championship win) + $157K (Super Bowl win) = $319,000 in playoff bonuses. That's before touching their regular season salary. For a rookie on the minimum contract ($750,000/year), $319,000 in playoff bonuses represents a 42% increase in annual income.

Beyond the Bonus: What Players Actually Earn

The $157,000 Super Bowl share grabs headlines, but context matters. Patrick Mahomes — the Chiefs' losing quarterback — earns $51 million in base salary for 2025. His $82,000 playoff loser share is roughly 0.16% of his annual earnings. For a practice squad player on the minimum wage, that same check is transformational. The NFL playoff bonus system is notable for being perfectly equal across the roster — a Super Bowl MVP and a third-string receiver get identical checks.
