Why The Players Championship Pays More Than the Majors

The Players Championship — often called "the fifth major" — is technically a PGA TOUR event, not a major. But its prize money has grown to surpass every major: $25 million in 2026 vs. $22.5 million at the Masters, $21.5 million at the US Open, and $20.5 million at the PGA Championship. The Tour funds it directly as its flagship event, using its greatest sponsorship leverage at TPC Sawgrass — one of the most recognizable courses in golf.

The Players vs. The Majors: Money Comparison

At $4.5 million, the winner's share at The Players exceeds that of every 2026 Major — including the PGA Championship ($3.69M) and the Masters ($4.5M, which it matches). It does not carry the same historical prestige, Fedex Cup multiplier, or lifetime exemption status as a major, but for one week at TPC Sawgrass, the financial stakes are uniquely high. The 17th hole island green has become golf's most famous par-3 precisely because of the drama — and the money — at stake.
