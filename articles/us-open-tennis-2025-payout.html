The US Open's Prize Money Dominance

The US Open has consistently led all Grand Slams in total prize money, and the 2025 edition extended that lead significantly. The $65 million total pool dwarfs Wimbledon's £53.5 million (~$72M in USD equivalent but varies by exchange), the Australian Open (~$75M AUD ≈ $48M USD), and Roland Garros (~€49M). In raw dollar terms, Flushing Meadows is the richest two-week stretch in tennis every year.

Importantly, the US Open has maintained strict prize money parity between men's and women's singles since 1973 — the first Grand Slam to do so. Both singles champions received the same $3.6 million.

Iga Świątek and the Women's Draw

Iga Świątek claimed her fourth US Open title in 2025, earning the $3.6 million top prize. For Świątek, consistent Grand Slam victories have made her one of the highest-earning women in sports — her US Open wins alone have generated over $12 million in prize money, before adding Roland Garros titles, WTA Finals earnings, and a comprehensive sponsorship portfolio including Rolex, Porsche, and others.
