Shohei Ohtani: The Most Unique Financial Story in Sports

Shohei Ohtani is the highest-earning player in baseball for the third time in four years — but almost entirely because of endorsements, not his playing contract. In 2026, the Los Angeles Dodgers pay him only $2 million in salary. The reason: when he signed his historic 10-year, $700 million contract in late 2023, Ohtani agreed to defer $68 million of his $70 million annual average — meaning the Dodgers pay him $680 million starting in 2034 ($68 million per year for 10 years), and just $2 million in cash salary during his playing years.

What makes up the gap? Endorsements. Ohtani's deal with roughly 20 brand partners — including JAL (Japan Airlines), Seiko, Hugo Boss, Kowa, Kosé, New Balance, and Fanatics — generated an estimated $125 million in 2026, setting a new record for endorsement earnings by any athlete in any sport. His total of $127 million tops the MLB earnings list by a wide margin despite his microscopic salary.

Juan Soto: The Largest Contract in Professional Sports History

In December 2024, Juan Soto signed a 15-year, $765 million deal with the New York Mets — the largest contract in the history of professional sports, surpassing Ohtani's $700 million Dodgers deal. The Soto contract includes a $75 million signing bonus and, critically, no deferred money — meaning the Mets pay every dollar on schedule, unlike Ohtani's arrangement. Soto also has an opt-out after the fifth year.

In 2026, Soto's cash salary is $61.9 million — the highest baseball salary in the league this year. Through the first two months of 2026, the Mets have assembled one of baseball's most expensive rosters, featuring Soto, Lindor, Wheeler, Bichette, and others in what owner Steve Cohen has committed to as a championship-or-bust approach.

The Deferred Money Era in Baseball

Ohtani's deal pioneered a new era in MLB contracts: massive deferred compensation. While MLB has always allowed deferred deals, no contract in history deferred so much: 97% of Ohtani's $700M is pushed back to 2034-2043. This structure benefits both parties — the Dodgers reduce their current payroll obligations, and Ohtani receives a guaranteed annuity-like structure that provides financial security beyond his playing years. For fans following current salaries, it creates the strange reality that Ohtani — statistically one of the best players in baseball — ranks 17th in actual 2026 cash salary at $2 million.

Back-to-Back Dodgers: Ohtani's World Series Impact

The Dodgers won consecutive World Series titles behind Ohtani in 2024 and 2025, validating their investment despite its unusual structure. Ohtani won the NL MVP for the third time in four seasons in 2025 and continues to be the most marketable athlete in American sports — combining Japanese market appeal (where his endorsement deals are particularly valuable) with American sports dominance. Back-to-back championships have supercharged his brand value, contributing to the record $125 million endorsement figure for 2026.
