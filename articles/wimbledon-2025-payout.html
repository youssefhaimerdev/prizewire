Wimbledon 2025: Full Prize Money Breakdown — Sinner Claims £3,000,000

Jannik Sinner won his first Wimbledon title in 2025, defeating defending champion Carlos Alcaraz in the final to claim £3,000,000 from a record total prize pool of £53,500,000 — a 7% increase over the previous year.

Full Prize Money Breakdown

The 2025 Championships at the All England Club set a new prize money record, with the total fund rising from £50 million to £53.5 million — representing an 11.1% increase for singles champions specifically. Jannik Sinner claimed his first Wimbledon crown, defeating world No. 2 Carlos Alcaraz in a rematch of their French Open final. For Alcaraz, who had won Wimbledon in 2023 and 2024, it was his first defeat in a Slam final. He collected £1,520,000 as runner-up.

Prize money at Wimbledon applies per round — not cumulatively. A player who reaches the semis earns £775,000 for that result, not the sum of all previous rounds. Players eliminated in qualifying receive between £15,500 and £41,500 depending on the qualifying round.

Doubles and Mixed Doubles Payouts

Men's and women's doubles champions each earned £680,000 per pair (a 4.4% increase from 2024). Mixed doubles winners received £135,000 per pair. Wheelchair singles champions took home £68,000 each.

How Wimbledon Compares to Other Grand Slams in 2025

Despite the record total, Wimbledon's singles winner prize of £3 million ($4.07M) still trails the US Open ($3.6M) and the Australian Open when converting currencies. The US Open 2025 had the largest total prize pool among the Grand Slams at $65 million. Roland Garros 2025 paid €2.4 million to the singles champion. Wimbledon remains notable for equal prize money across men's and women's draws — a standard the tournament has maintained since 2007.
