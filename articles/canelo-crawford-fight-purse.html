The Most Financially Lopsided Boxing Upset of 2025

On September 13, 2025, Terence Crawford walked into Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas as the 10-1 underdog — and walked out the undisputed super middleweight champion of the world. He defeated Canelo Álvarez by unanimous decision in a performance that stunned the boxing world. The financial gap between the two fighters was almost as staggering as the result itself.

Turki Alalshikh, chairman of Saudi Arabia's General Entertainment Authority and head of Riyadh Season, confirmed at the pre-fight press conference that Canelo was making "more than $100 million" for the fight. Reports placed the figure at $100-150 million — part of Canelo's reported $400 million multi-fight deal with Riyadh Season signed in early 2025. Crawford, by contrast, revealed on a podcast that he received $10 million — roughly 6-10% of Canelo's payday — saying: "I'm doing it for the opportunity, baby — the legacy."

Boxing vs UFC: The Pay Gap in One Fight

The Canelo-Crawford combined payout of approximately $200 million nearly matched the estimated total disclosed earnings of the UFC's entire roster for 2025 ($210 million). That comparison — two boxers vs. hundreds of MMA fighters — became the most-discussed fighter pay topic of the year, reigniting debate about how UFC structures compensation versus boxing's free-market system. Crawford himself highlighted the contrast: "That's why UFC champs all want to box — way more money to be had at the top."

Canelo's Career Earnings Approach $800 Million

The Crawford fight pushed Canelo's career earnings from fight purses and endorsements to an estimated $800 million, according to Sportico — making him one of the highest-earning athletes in sports history. This places him alongside Floyd Mayweather, Tiger Woods, and Michael Jordan in the all-time earnings elite. At 35, Canelo remains under contract with Riyadh Season for several more fights, meaning his career total could approach or surpass $1 billion before retirement.
