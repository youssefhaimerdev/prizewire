Kylian Mbappé Salary at Real Madrid: €100M/Year + €150M Signing Bonus

Kylian Mbappé joined Real Madrid on a free transfer from PSG in July 2024, signing one of the most lucrative contracts in football history — including a reported €150 million signing bonus and approximately €100 million per year in total package.

The Transfer That Changed Football Finance

When Kylian Mbappé signed for Real Madrid in July 2024, it completed one of the longest-running transfer sagas in football history — and triggered one of the largest financial commitments a football club has ever made to an individual player. Mbappé's contract was widely reported to include a €150 million signing bonus, despite him arriving as a free agent when his PSG contract expired. That bonus alone exceeded the transfer fees Real Madrid had paid for Cristiano Ronaldo (€94M, 2009) and Gareth Bale (€100M, 2013).

His annual salary package — including base salary, image rights payments, and performance bonuses — is estimated at approximately €100 million per year. This makes Mbappé the highest-paid player in Real Madrid's history and the third-highest-paid footballer on earth behind Cristiano Ronaldo (~€200M) and Lionel Messi ($70-80M total).

What the Signing Bonus Means for PSG

The €150M signing bonus generated significant controversy. French courts later ordered PSG to pay Mbappé €60 million in withheld wages and bonuses from his final season at the club — a legal battle that ran parallel to his Madrid move and underscored the bitter nature of the separation. PSG had previously attempted to block Mbappé's departure and withheld payments in what became one of football's most acrimonious transfer situations.
