The Most Expensive Grid in F1 History

The 2026 Formula 1 season features a combined driver salary bill of approximately $324 million across all 11 constructors — the highest in the sport's history. That figure represents a 23% increase from 2024 and reflects both rising TV revenues, record race attendance, and the new commercial agreements negotiated under F1's Concorde Agreement. The average F1 driver salary in 2026 is $14.7 million — but that average is heavily skewed by the top five contracts, which account for over 55% of the total bill.

The most extraordinary figure in the grid is not the top — it's the contrast. Max Verstappen earns $70 million. His teammate Liam Lawson earns $2 million. The championship leader, Kimi Antonelli, earns $2 million base — on the same grid, in many cases finishing ahead of drivers earning 35x his salary.

Antonelli: The $2M Championship Leader

The defining financial story of the 2026 season is the inversion at the top of the championship. Kimi Antonelli — 19 years old, four wins in five races, leading the WDC standings — earns $2 million base. Max Verstappen — currently third in the championship after a difficult start to the new regulations — earns $70 million. The gap between the championship leader and the highest-paid driver is $68 million. F1 has never seen anything quite like it.

Antonelli's contract runs through 2027. His bonuses could push total 2026 earnings to $14 million if he wins the championship. But the renegotiation conversations will begin the moment the 2026 season concludes — and if Antonelli is world champion in November, his next contract will place him firmly among the top five earners on the grid.

The Verstappen Exit Clause

One of the most financially consequential subplot of 2026 is Max Verstappen's reported exit clause at Red Bull. Multiple credible sources have reported that his contract contains a performance clause — if he is outside P2 in the championship standings at the summer break, a release clause becomes active. With Antonelli dominating and Verstappen struggling to adapt to the new regulations, that clause is a genuine discussion point entering the Monaco weekend. Red Bull and Verstappen have not publicly confirmed the details.
