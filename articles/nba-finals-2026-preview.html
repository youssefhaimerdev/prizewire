The Eastern Conference: New York Knicks Are Back

The New York Knicks are in the NBA Finals for the first time since 1999, when they lost to the San Antonio Spurs in five games. This time, they swept the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Finals — a dominant performance that earned Jalen Brunson the ECF MVP award. The Garden erupted. Twenty-seven years of waiting ended in four games.

Brunson's rise has been one of the great stories of this NBA era. A second-round pick who wasn't supposed to be a franchise player, he has become the unquestioned leader of one of basketball's most storied franchises. His ECF performance — averaging approximately 32 points per game in the sweep — made the MVP award unanimous. The Knicks also received standout contributions from OG Anunoby, Karl-Anthony Towns, Mikal Bridges, and Jalen Williams.

NBA Finals Prize Money: Full Breakdown

The 2026 NBA playoff prize pool totals $24.6 million, distributed across all 16 playoff teams based on how far they advance. The Finals champion earns $547,000 per player — nearly $400,000 more per player than an NFL Super Bowl winner. Every player on the winning roster receives the same amount regardless of minutes played or contract size.

Who Has More to Gain — Brunson or Wembanyama?

If the Spurs make the Finals, Wembanyama's $547,000 championship share would be just 4% of his $13.4 million season salary — a rounding error compared to the contract extension waiting for him on July 6. For Brunson, a championship would be the capstone on one of basketball's great underdog stories. Financially, both players are already set for life. But the NBA Finals — and the Larry O'Brien Trophy — means something beyond money.
