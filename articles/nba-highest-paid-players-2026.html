The NBA's Pay Scale in 2026

NBA salaries in 2026 are governed by the Collective Bargaining Agreement, the salary cap, and maximum contract rules. Unlike the NFL — where contracts are often not fully guaranteed — NBA contracts are fully guaranteed from the moment they are signed. This means every player on this list is guaranteed to collect their listed salary even if they are injured or released.

The salary cap for 2025-26 is approximately $140 million, with a luxury tax threshold of approximately $170 million. Teams that spend above the luxury tax line pay a dollar-for-dollar penalty (and more) on each dollar spent over the line. Teams like the Golden State Warriors, LA Lakers, and New York Knicks regularly pay luxury tax — meaning they effectively spend $200M+ to field their rosters when tax is included.

The Wembanyama Anomaly

The most striking figure on any NBA salary list in 2026 is Victor Wembanyama's $13.4 million — which ranks him approximately 30th in the league despite the fact that he is leading the San Antonio Spurs to the Western Conference Finals and is widely considered a top-3 player in the NBA. His rookie scale contract was set before anyone knew how dominant he would become. On July 6, 2026, he becomes eligible for a rookie maximum extension worth approximately $255-303 million over five years. His extension will likely place him in the top 5 earners in the league by 2027-28.

Stephen Curry: Still Getting Paid at the Top

At 37 years old, Stephen Curry continues to lead the league in base salary at $55.76 million. Curry signed a four-year, $215 million extension with Golden State in 2021 — the first player ever to sign a deal worth more than $200 million. Despite his age, Curry remains one of basketball's best players and biggest commercial draws. His total annual earnings — including endorsement deals with Under Armour, JP Morgan Chase, FTX predecessor brands, and others — push his real annual income well above $100 million.

The Knicks' Bet: Brunson + Taxes

Jalen Brunson's $45.6 million salary is just the beginning of the New York Knicks' payroll commitment. Factor in OG Anunoby, Karl-Anthony Towns, Mikal Bridges, and their supporting cast — plus luxury tax penalties — and the Knicks are spending well over $250 million for their 2026 NBA Finals roster. For ownership led by Steve Ballmer... wait, that's the Clippers. The Knicks, owned by James Dolan's MSG, are spending at championship-or-bust levels for the first time in decades. The NBA Finals appearance justifies it.
