How prize money works in every major sport

NFL Playoff Prize Money

The NFL distributes playoff prize money through a structured per-player bonus system negotiated annually. Players earn incremental bonuses at each round — Wild Card, Divisional, Conference Championship, and Super Bowl — on top of their base salary. The Super Bowl winner's player share is the largest per-player bonus in American team sports.

For 2025–26, the NFL's total playoff pool exceeds $120 million. The Super Bowl LIX winner's share was $157,000 per player. That number only captures the disclosed NFL bonus — roster incentives, performance clauses, and signing bonuses can substantially increase a player's actual event earnings.

  • Super Bowl Winner Share $157,000/player
  • Super Bowl Loser Share $82,000/player
  • Conf. Championship Win $67,000/player
  • Divisional Round Win $54,000/player
  • Wild Card Win $41,000/player
  • Total Playoff Pool 2025–26$120,000,000

NBA Playoff Prize Money

NBA playoff bonuses are distributed from a central prize pool split by round and seeding. Unlike salary cap figures — which are governed by the CBA — playoff bonuses are fixed each season by the league. The championship roster typically earns over $540,000 per player in playoff bonus money.

The 2025–26 NBA playoff prize pool totals $24.6 million. First-round exits receive the smallest cut, while the champion's roster takes the largest share. These figures don't include the significant salary earnings players receive during playoff games per their existing contracts.

PGA TOUR Prize Money

PGA TOUR purses have grown dramatically, driven by competitive pressure from LIV Golf and rising media rights revenue. The four Major championships carry the greatest prestige, while The Players Championship commands the largest individual purse on Tour at $25 million in 2026.

TOUR purse distribution follows a standard structure: the winner earns approximately 18% of total purse. Players who miss the cut receive nothing. Purses scale dramatically — a $21M purse like the PGA Championship yields $3.78M to the winner, while 70th place earns roughly $22,000.

  • The Players Champ. 2026 $25,000,000 purse
  • PGA Championship 2026 $21,000,000 purse
  • US Open Golf 2025 $21,500,000 purse
  • The Open 2025 $17,000,000 purse

Formula 1 Prize Money

Formula 1 runs one of the most complex prize fund structures in all of sports. FOM (Formula One Management) distributes over $1.1 billion annually to constructors based on their standings, historical bonuses, and performance. Individual drivers don't receive FOM funds directly — that money goes to the constructors.

The World Constructors' Championship winner earns approximately $147 million. Even the last-placed constructor takes home roughly $35 million — one reason teams remain financially viable despite spending hundreds of millions on operations and development.

  • Total 2026 Prize Fund ~$1,100,000,000
  • WCC 1st Place ~$147,000,000

Tennis Prize Money

Professional tennis distributes prize money across a tiered structure spanning ATP, WTA, and Grand Slam events. The four Slams carry the largest purses, with the US Open leading at over $65 million total in 2025. Prize money parity between men's and women's singles has been achieved at all four Grand Slams.

The ATP Finals (season-ending championship) carries a $9.75 million prize pool. Wimbledon's historic total purse reached £50 million in 2025, with the singles champion earning £3.18 million. Prize money scales steeply by round — first-round exits earn a fraction of what semifinals pay.

  • US Open 2025 Total $65,000,000
  • US Open Singles Winner $3,600,000
  • Wimbledon 2025 Total £50,000,000
  • Wimbledon Singles Winner £3,180,000
  • ATP Finals 2025 Total $9,750,000

UFC Fighter Pay

UFC fighter compensation is notoriously opaque. State athletic commissions require disclosed purse amounts, but these figures often exclude undisclosed bonuses, PPV points, and performance incentives that top fighters receive. The disclosed purse is frequently only a fraction of total fight-night earnings.

The UFC issues four standard performance bonuses per event — Fight of the Night and two Performance of the Night awards — each worth $50,000. Championship-level fighters can earn undisclosed bonuses bringing total compensation well into the millions, but those figures aren't publicly confirmed.

  • UFC 315 Disclosed Total $3,400,000
  • Main Event Winner (Disc.) $1,050,000
  • Main Event Loser (Disc.) $525,000
  • Co-Main Winner (Disc.) $300,000
  • Performance of the Night $50,000 each
  • Fight of the Night $50,000 each
